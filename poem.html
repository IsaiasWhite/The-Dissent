The Dissent

A collection of Poetry from the Author

Are You Available

Part of the mass, now singled to your own piece
Stood with the group, but now you’re on your own feet
There was a time when no one seemed to leave you
No it seems, no one tends to see you
No longer identified with the world’s people
If they mistreat you, they did not commit a crime
Or violate your rights, because you’re not seen as equal
And you’re not, because of what’s in the inside
It’s like a star, because it heats as it shines
But it doesn’t need a stage to bring attention to it’s light
You won’t blaze for the moment, and burnout by the morning
But wax for the torn, and shed for the mourning
This isn’t ordinary, to most intimidating
That you of no value exceeded expectations
If they won’t detain you, self will restrain you
But none can stop the truth, if you’re available

All have the pine in their eyes to be great
But can you lionize the lives soaked in strife
To toss a lifeline in the seas made of their crying
Be a spark to the flame of the soul for their drying
Most of us just aspire
As long as we keep eating, other’s lives can expire
But I feel we come to a choice in our lives
To be glutenous in self, or stop eating the plate of lies
Fasting from self, to try and fasten yourself
To a diet that is fashioned for health
Cease from living through appetites, to ample life
Beyond the desires to postpone death/
Claiming you’re living to the fullest, sounds foolish
When nothing trickles down during your constant moving
Leaving the vessels under you half empty and destitute
To the brim you weren’t filled, cause space wasn’t available

- Isaiah White

More To Life

There’s more to life than what we see right before us.
Getting caught in “could’s” and “would’s”, it’ll get as thick as a forest.
There’s more to life than the ability to touch.
Because once that thing is gone, you won’t feel much.
There’s more to life than things that are satisfying.
As long as you have eyes, you won’t fill the voids in your iris.
There’s more to life than bodies that attract.
If it doesn’t know love, then it won’t love back.
Which means there’s more to life than knowing one another.
If I don’t know me, how could I know another.
But there’s more to life than the existence of your views.
Wisdom is not gained from disclosing your own truth.
And, there’s more to life than the life of your struggles.
All have strife, and none is worse than another’s.
Not to blind you, just trying to shed light
On the truth, that there’s more to life.

There’s more to life than all the worlds cash.
It can’t buy love, peace, and grace that’ll last.
There’s more to life than women that are fast.
Because at the right speed, you’re sure to lose track.
There’s more to life than having a lot of stones.
Possibly all the ice may make one emotionless.
So, there’s more to life than being the coolest.
Because the hottest trends are growing cold.
There’s more to life than trying to please people.
If you favor one, the others feel unequal.
So, there’s more to life than what they say.
Keep your eyes focused and follow what’s been paved.
There’s more to life than the world’s deafness.
You know you heard a word, they don’t want you to listen.
They may frown because you shine too bright,
But show them that there’s more to life.

- Isaiah White

Loiterer

I've been around here for a minute.
You thought I left, but I'm still up in this.
Real adversaries will always tarry
For your demise, if they are living.
I have some work that I haven't finished.
I am the hurt of you daily grimace.
You've been distracted, I've been relaxing
Because you believe there's bigger business.
Because you perceive there's better interest.
And you grieve in their disappearance.
But, their importance is a distortion
Of your life and the way you live it.
Maybe you can see me very vivid.
But, you are scared of my existence.
So, you cover up with the other stuff
To take your mind off your condition.
Trying to act like we're not conflicting,
  And I'm not really fucking with your feelings.
But to get rid of THIS, you must be intimate
With yourself and with the enemy.
Or, you too will become a villain.
  Viewing the world with a very cynic
Type of mindset, thinking you found it
Because you express your hurt in excess.
Believing a cold heart gave you access
To all the facts of Life's secrets.
  But, that's not possible without control.
See, your revelations are from my genesis.
Every step you took, I was the cause of it.
Everything you made, I was the elements.
I devoured all of the power
The very hour that you neglected me.
So, sweep me under the rug.
The dirt will soon pile up.
I know how big your closet is.
There's not enough room for the skeletons.
Because you share things in marriages,
And bondage is what this marriage is.
Don't you think that I'll just pack and leave.
I'll wear this ring until you get rid of me.
... So face me.

- Isaiah White

The Jewel

It’s the only thing I have to give.
It’s the only reason I rise to live.
It’s the greater part of my identity.
It’s because of this you notice me.
I was confused of its existence.
Thought it was something that needed seeking.
Thought it would be earned with my labor,
But it’s my work to share it with my neighbor.
Daily, it was perfected.
For the moments to share it, in discretion.
But Wisdom says give it unfiltered,
So that at my lowest others are lifted.
So, this is the last judgement.
A death sentence to future grudges.
The new law under the present verdict.
The best of me, regardless if you deserve it.

- Isaiah White

All Good

Good is life, and good is love.
Gift the good. Oh, and good trust.
Good is right, and goodness knows
That good is light, and good is hope.
Things progress, when goodness is shown.
That is truth, because progression is growth.
When hidden, goodness remains.
Some things are good riddance, if they refuse change.
Good is balance, when you are challenged.
Allow the good in you to overwhelm malice.
Don’t manage goodness, it flows unhindered.
If it’s truly good, it needs no mentor.
It’s good to learn. Goodness will teach.
It’s good to show, because goodness will seek.
And, if you are closed goodness will breach.
But if you choose to deny, goodness will leave.

- Isaiah White

Pushed

Let me know that I’m alive,
By brining me through this pain.
Let me know that I’m alive,
Because all I’ve been feeling is drained.
All men that’ve died,
Have lost this joy of being hurt.
So I’ll walk in honor of this curse,
Inspired by what they’ve gained.
If liars, cheaters, abusers and beaters,
And all of my problems gave way.
Would I exist? How could I exist,
If that that strain is a sign of my change?
And, that change is a symbol of growth.
Without growth we’d just wither and wane.
So help me believe, when I receive
The feeling of living again.

- Isaiah White

Leave Your Masters

As we travel, we guard it heavily.
Allowing it to enter situations with preconceived
Notions, treading lakes, seas, and oceans
As if every body is the same.
Though some are salty and some are salt free,
We’re heels high fighting the grain, for honor of its name.
Swimming for two, you tire soon and go deep.
Holding this grudge, you never give up.
Now, its retardment has you exhausted.
And, neither of you has the ability to breathe.
As your grip softens and you become freed,
In your disbelief, the water feels so serene.
This body, unlike another’s, lifts the untethered.
You’re rising to the top, because at this time you shouldn’t speak.
As you ascend higher, don’t regret letting that load sink.
Gather composure as you emerge from the leagues,
But don’t be so sure of it as you swim towards it.
Keep in mind that you’ve only broken the surface.
In your struggle, that was a lot you swallowed.
Reach humbly in your wade, leaving love in your wake.

- Isaiah White

Attempt

Out of the blocks, into the drive phase.
Keep your head down and bound to get away.
Farther from the start, with every step we take.
But it’s a whole race before we break the tape,
And experience the gifts of this sealed box.
Some will start and before their cut, stop.
Though no one has to be disqualified,
Some will drop themselves, spiritual suicide.
It’s your decision to put your shoes aside.
But if you don’t run, you still chose a side.
That’s for those who presume they’ll get by,
Just because they didn’t decide to fight.
And, attempt to endure to the finish.
Stride for life, not to be in the Guinness.
Yet, some feel bliss is in ignorance.
But ignoring a problem, means you’re still in it.

- Isaiah White

Stumped

Do I stress to express the things that I believe?
Should I focus on the way it’ll be perceived?
I know it’s more important to the roots of the tree,
Than it is to guard the dignity of every leaf.
Debate the times and seasons from the colors displayed.
Wasting breath reasoning of why they didn’t stray.
But, simply deployed.
What you thought was falling to die,
Was to spread joy.
Not in the sense of a martyr.
Decay isn’t the only fertilizer.
As fertilizing may happen through the living,
Autumn’s downfall may not be what it seems.
As she’s stripped away of what’s been given.
We call her finished, but she means new beginning.
Understand you won’t grow anew,
Until you share your present fruit.
If the gift wastes out of fearing their taste,
Is there a point of protecting you?

- Isaiah White

Surge

Take charge of my life,
And replenish what is lost.
Excite my soul out of this ground state.
My mind will follow to higher clouds.
And, my eyes won’t tend to depend on what is now.
Often drained after living in these moments.
Just to find, this source runs dry.
To become barren and no longer sustained.
I know the outlet I need for life.
Lightning’s Creator, better than generators
That are used to power cities.
But they don’t last forever.
And if they did, wouldn’t fill me.

- Isaiah White

No More Suffering in Silence

From the beginning I’m praying for my insolence
Forgiveness for these words that are coming from my feelings
But please don’t ignore the rants of my ignorance
What I vent is the steam I’m tired of covering
Loneliness, hopelessness, the time between messages
Enemy attacks that makes it hard to keep focus with
The call that was made for me
I see nothing forming, though I’ve put my heart in it
My neck has grown weary, I’m not trying to look down
Waiting to rise upon wings, while being wise like the owl
Perched on my porch, tempted to stoop down
None of the kids play with the stoop child
These thoughts are hard to post, I decided to mail them
But sure enough they were returned to cinder
Was told a flame starts from glowing embers
Keeping my heart warm and my mind kindled
Brilliant in the depths of life’s amazon
So I can see underneath the trees’ canopy
It gets dark down here by the roots
What’s to expect when you really search for you
Digging deeper than the bark, until I tap the sap
It started so bitter, but I can make syrup from that
I mean telling my strife through a man’s fear of tears
Showing when life is bitter, it gets sweeter if you persevere
*In loving memory of Keyth and the Talley family*

- Isaiah White
